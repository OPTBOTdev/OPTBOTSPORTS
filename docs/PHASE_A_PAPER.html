The Environment Tower: an identity-free neural encoder of hockey context

OptBot Phase A — design rationale, full specification, and honest limits · July 2026 · companion to ARCHITECTURE_PLAN.md v2.2

Abstract. A hockey player's observed statistics are a blend of who he is and what surrounded him — linemates, opponents, coach, score, zone, fatigue, rink. Any system that projects a player onto a NEW team must separate the two, because the whole question is "keep the player, swap the surroundings." Phase A is a small (~2 M parameter) neural network with one job: compress everything that surrounds a player during a stretch of ice time into a single vector, h_env, while provably knowing nothing about the player himself. This paper explains why the encoder must be identity-free, what it eats and why each input earned its place, how it trains, how it interacts with Phase B (the combiner) and Phase C (the player side), what can still go wrong, and — candidly — where it aligns with and where it is not required by the October MVP proof.

1. The problem, stated precisely

We want, for player p, destination environment E′, and decision time t₀:

theta(p, E′, t₀) = E[ first-40-game on-ice impact | do(E ~ E′), history(p, <t₀) ]

Read it aloud: the expected on-ice impact over his first ~40 games, if we surgically set his environment to E′, given only what was knowable about him before t₀. The do(·) is the whole company. Ordinary stats models learn "players who happened to be in good environments produce more" — an association. We need "what happens when we place this player into that environment" — an intervention. The difference is why a model can be excellent at prediction and useless for a trade decision.

For the intervention to be computable, the model must factor into pieces we can swap independently. That forces the three-part shape:

outcome ≈ g( h_env(E),  talent(p),  levers(usage) ) — three pipes, never entangled

Phase A builds h_env. Phase C builds talent. Phase B is g. This paper is about the first pipe, but §6–7 show the joints, because the tower's design decisions only make sense in light of what the other two pipes require of it.

2. The central design decision: why the tower must be identity-free

The single most important sentence in this paper: the tower never sees the player it is describing the ice for. No ID, no stats, no priors, no deployment history. Three architectures were considered; two were rejected.

2a. Rejected: the monolith

One network eating player + environment + usage, predicting outcomes. It would predict well — and be unusable. With everything entangled, "swap the team" has no handle to pull: the network's internal representation of McDavid is partly a representation of Draisaitl and of Edmonton's pace, and no one can say which part. Swapping inputs produces an answer, but not the answer to the causal question — the estimated effect of the environment is confounded by every player who selected into it.

2b. Rejected: the firewalled two-stream (our own previous design)

The legacy Phase A (still on disk as encoder.py) put a focal-player identity stream and a context stream in one module, with a one-way attention "firewall" so context could peek at a detached identity summary. The audit verdict: firewalls of this kind are promises, not proofs. Gradient paths are blockable; information paths through shared batch statistics, through co-adapted training dynamics, and through the sheer presence of the focal embedding in the same module are not. And every leak is invisible until it manifests as an overconfident wrong answer on exactly the rows we sell — movers. We deprecated the design (July 2026) rather than audit it forever: the cheapest firewall is absence.

2c. Chosen: absence, plus a separate talent channel

The tower's inputs are the surroundings only. The player enters the system once, in Phase B, through a pre-computed, frozen scalar channel (§7). Now "swap the environment" is literal: recompute h_env with the destination's people and context; the talent numbers do not move. The intervention is an actual code path, not an interpretation.

The subtle residual threat (T-fingerprint). Identity can re-enter through the back door: five teammate IDs nearly identify the team, the line, and often the focal player himself (who else centers Draisaitl and Hyman?). A tower that learns "this specific WITH-set ⇒ elite production" has partially memorized McDavid without ever seeing his ID. v2.3 UPDATE — this threat is now structurally ELIMINATED, not mitigated (the OXF stack, ADR-001, adopted after a 4-designer / 14-attack adversarial panel): (1) orthogonalized labels — every training outcome has the focal player's own estimated contribution subtracted (unshrunk, split-sample), so guessing the missing man pays ZERO reward anywhere; (2) player-fold cross-fitting, shared-nothing — the tower AND trunk that score you never trained on a single row where you were the hidden man; (3) synthetic teammate dropout DELETED — masking a real contributor while his effect stayed in the outcome was quietly training the fingerprint; (4) leave-focal-out team priors + pairwise-LOO member scalars close the echo side doors. Probes remain as falsification tests (a red probe files a bug, never a re-roll). The honest floor: glued duos (~zero apart-minutes) are unidentifiable by ANY method — those counterfactuals get flagged extrapolated or refused, never guessed. Full derivation: phase_a_paper.pdf §2.4 + ADR-001_fingerprint_elimination.md.

3. What the tower eats — every input, and why it earned its seat

The governing law (enforced in code by contracts/tower_schema.py, which refuses unclassified features at import): every input must be either exactly constructible for a hypothetical destination from pre-t₀ data (SYNTH), or servable as a distribution via the slot bootstrap (MARG), or it is BANNED. The test is always serving-time honesty: could we produce this number on trade day, for a trade that hasn't happened?

4. The architecture itself

4a. Member representation: static core ⊕ dynamic shell

member_j = MLP( id_vec_j(96) ⊕ shell_j ) where shell_j = [age, pos, hand, lagged_form, member_gsc]

Why the split: identity vectors capture what's stable about a player (skill signature as seen from outside); the shell captures what changes (aging, form, settling). This split is also the UNK mode: a player the table has never seen — an AHL call-up today; an entire AHL roster in the future recruiting product — gets the UNK vector plus a REAL shell. The tower degrades gracefully from "I know this man" to "I know his type," which is precisely the future-product path (§9).

4b. Pooling: seconds-biased attention

attn_logit_j = q·member_j + log(seconds_j) → h_with = Σ softmax_j · member_j

Why not a mean (treats a 3-second cameo like a full shift), why not max (one member dominates), why not plain attention (learns to over-focus, and can resurrect the fingerprint threat): the log(seconds) bias makes exposure the prior and lets attention adjust around it. Padding (id 0, seconds 0) is masked exactly — audited: padding conventions hold in 100% of 200k sampled windows.

4c. Context and fusion

h_ctx = MLP( embeds(score, zone, period, rink, coach, …) ⊕ team_priors ⊕ schedule ) h_env = Fusion( [h_with, h_vs, h_ctx] + CLS ) — 2 pre-norm attention blocks → 256-d

Fusion is attention (not concatenation) because context should modulate how the people matter: the same five opponents mean something different protecting a lead in the third than chasing in the first. Two blocks, not six: with ~1,700 identity vectors as the main learnable inventory, capacity is the enemy (§5c).

4d. Size: ~2M parameters, and why small is a feature

The identity table (~1.7k × 96) plus MLPs and two fusion blocks lands near 2M. The constraint is sample efficiency per PLAYER, not per row: 6.5M windows sounds enormous, but each identity vector is learned from only that player's appearances. A big tower would memorize windows; a small one is forced to learn hockey. (The GBDT twin in Phase B exists to referee exactly this: if a gradient-boosted tree on the tabular features matches the trunk, the neural machinery isn't earning its keep.)

5. Training — and every trap we know about

5a. No loss of its own

The tower trains end-to-end with the Phase B trunk against window outcomes. "Environment quality" is not a thing we define; it's whatever representation makes the trunk's predictions right across two million windows. This is deliberate: any hand-built environment target (e.g. "predict slot-average xG") would cap the tower at reproducing our old averages. The price: every Phase B pathology can reach the tower through the gradient — which is why the gradient PLUMBING is part of the architecture (5b–5d).

5b. The weighting stack (who gets a vote)

MechanismWhat it prevents
exposure weights (loss × seconds)low-minute windows dominating: a 6-second rate observation is ~1/100th the vote of a 10-minute one
heteroscedastic heads (predict mean AND variance)noisy windows steering: the Gaussian NLL divides error by predicted σ² — the model learns short windows are noisy and automatically mutes them
switch-weighting ×3 on post-move windowsthe regime we sell being drowned by the settled majority. Safe now BECAUSE gsc is an explicit input (F-2): the adjustment is modeled, so upweighting doesn't smear it
is_multistint downweightblended-row noise from multi-shift windows

5d. Era and evaluation hygiene

Era-split checkpoints (≤2021 / 22 / 23 / 24); all ablation comparisons hold era fixed (B-3) — otherwise "the tower wins" can be an artifact of which seasons a run saw. Teammate dropout 20% doubles as augmentation and anti-fingerprint. 2018-19 currently trains in shell-only mode for member form (no prior season on the surface yet — fills when the 2017-18 build lands; if it stalls, that season is excluded rather than trained on a hole).

6. The joint with Phase B (the combiner)

x = [ h_env(256) ⊕ talent_channel ⊕ bio ] → 4×768 SwiGLU residual trunk, FiLM-modulated by 17 levers → 10 heads

6a. Three pipes, physically separate

Environment (from A), talent (from C, frozen — gradients do not flow into talent), levers (the coach's controllable knobs: zone-start mix, matchup exposure…) enter through FiLM modulation, meaning the levers RESHAPE the computation rather than add to it — that's what lets us later answer "what if we used him differently?" without retraining. The separation is the product: "how good he is" can never trade places with "how he was used."

6b. Ten heads, one licensed ruler

Primary: MoneyPuck xG for/against (heteroscedastic Gaussian) + rush/rebound/chaos splits. Auxiliary witness: our in-house xG (agreement between rulers is a free consistency check). Counts (goals/shots) as Poisson with exposure offsets. Kendall-Gal uncertainty weighting balances the heads without hand-tuning.

6c. The T19 firewall that IS legitimate

Micro-stat heads (hits/giveaways/takeaways) train with gradients detached from the tower: arena scorers disagree by up to 2.25× on giveaways, and that noise may not sculpt h_env. (Contrast with §2b: we distrust firewalls protecting against identity, because identity leaks are subtle and adversarial; a gradient-detach against scorer noise is a one-line mechanical guarantee — the gradient literally does not exist. Different threat class, different confidence.)

6d. Two gates, two questions

The TOWER's gate: beat the slot-average environment (v0's crude average) in era-fixed ablation — does knowing the specific humans help? The TRUNK's gate: beat the GBDT twin — does the neural combiner beat a strong tabular baseline given the same inputs? Conflating these was an early design bug; they answer different questions and both must pass.

7. The joint with Phase C (the player side)

7a. Talent as a residual — the engine of the proven 7.6%

talent(p) = EB-shrink( decay-weighted Σ [what p's windows produced − what the BASELINE says an average player produces in those exact windows] )

Career-long, recency-decayed, league-demeaned (so league-wide drift doesn't masquerade as personal decline), shrunk toward zero when evidence is thin (fitted K in effective minutes — the 40-minute wonder cannot carry an extreme number anywhere). Computed out-of-fold: the baseline never saw the windows it judges.

7b. The double-count theorem, and K-1

If the environment model is identity-blind, the residual "talent" of a player contains credit that actually belongs to his linemates. If you then upgrade the environment model to one that DOES credit linemates (the tower), and keep the old residuals, the same value is counted twice.

Concretely: v0's baseline doesn't know Draisaitl is elite, so some Draisaitl credit lives inside McDavid-linemates' talent scalars. In v0, the errors partially cancel (environment underclaims, talent overclaims). Ship the tower with old talent and the cancellation breaks: systematic overprediction for star-adjacent players leaving their stars — the exact GM question ("is he real without 97?") answered wrong, with confidence. Hence ship-gate K-1: before v1 ships, talent is RE-DERIVED as the residual against the TOWER's environment (one EM round: train tower → recompute residuals → verify the top/bottom-15 sniff still ranks hockey-sensibly). Non-negotiable.

7c. The v2 frontier (not in the MVP)

Phase C grows a style vector (rush sniper / net-front / point bomber — the 13,708 pre-computed shot-personality cards are the raw material) which, interacted with the tower's people-vectors, becomes the chemistry/fit engine ("does a triggerman need a distributor?"). Gated behind an identifiability census — chemistry claims require enough co-occurrence data to be estimable, or they're astrology.

8. Serving: how a hypothetical becomes a number

Query: player → destination team → line/role. The scenario builder takes the destination slot's REAL recent windows (who was actually on that ice), substitutes the focal player's talent in Phase B, bootstraps ×2000 over those windows weighted by the JOINT future schedule (opponent, venue, rest — never independent marginals: no Frankenstein windows that never co-occur), integrates over the settling curve (games 1..40 via gsc), and emits: projected on-ice xGF% + a conformal band whose coverage was MEASURED on 860 historical moves (80.0% achieved vs 80% claimed), binned by evidence volume × intended-role-delta (the K-2 fix: a 3rd-liner queried as a 1st-liner draws his band from the wide-change error pool, not the average pool). Combinations outside the data's joint support get an explicit extrapolated: true; far outside → REFUSED_OOS. Refusal is a feature: it is what makes the other answers credible.

9. MVP alignment — and honest disalignment

The MVP (agreed July 2026): enough proof to land an NHL team partnership or seed funding. Concretely: the engine (v0 already beats Marcel-class baselines by 7.6%, CI clear), sealed September predictions, the public scoreboard, and the market-calls log (gated by its own backtest). One target, one event, one raise.

9a. Where Phase A serves that goal

  • It is the upgrade path with a floor. The tower ships ONLY if it beats v0 on the same 860-move harness, CI-clear, all 7 gates. October's proof does not wait for it and cannot be hurt by it: worst case v0 ships and the tower iterates.
  • It is the demo moment. v0 can't answer "and what if he plays with Kane instead?" — the tower can (linemate overrides). That interactivity is what makes a GM demo feel like an engine, not a spreadsheet.
  • It is the differentiation claim. "First causal simulation engine in sports" needs do()-capable machinery — §1's factorization IS that machinery, defensible to a technical diligence partner.
  • It compounds into the expansion story. UNK/shell mode (§4a) is the AHL recruiting product's front door; the same tower serves both pitches.

9b. Where Phase A does NOT serve the MVP (read this part too)

  • October does not require it. The sealed predictions can be v0's. If GPU access slips past ~mid-August, training a tower that must clear 7 gates before sealing gets tight — and the correct decision is to seal v0 and keep training. The plan already says this; saying it out loud here keeps us honest.
  • The market-calls leg doesn't use it. SOG-prop translation runs on shot cards + role minutes + v0 environment; win-totals on roster xG-churn. The Vegas proof is deliberately tower-independent (fewer moving parts under a deadline).
  • It adds the K-1 obligation. Shipping the tower forces the talent re-derivation and re-validation. That's a cost v0 never charges. It's paid once, by design, but it is real days.
  • Its risk is real: the honest prior is that the tower beats slot-average comfortably but beats v0's harness number by a MODEST margin first try (v0 is crude on environment but its talent channel — the thing that carries most signal — is identical). The gates exist because "neural = better" is a hypothesis, not an entitlement.

10. Questions you should grill me with (the reader's checklist)

  1. "If the tower never sees the player, how can the projection be about HIM?" — Because Phase B injects him through the talent channel. The tower answers "what is this ice worth to a generic player"; talent shifts it to him. Chemistry (whether THIS ice suits THIS style) is v2 — ask me what we lose by deferring it.
  2. "Five teammate IDs basically name the focal player. Why isn't that fatal?" — Dropout + P3-incremental probe + K-1. Ask to see the probe numbers on the first real checkpoint.
  3. "Why is teammate form allowed at all if it echoes him?" — Season-lagged only; measured residual correlation is real assortativity. Exact pairwise leave-focal-out is the v1.1 upgrade; ask what it costs.
  4. "What if the tower just fails?" — v0 ships. The company never depends on the net. That is the point of the harness.
  5. "Why should I believe the bands?" — Because they were measured: 80.0% achieved on 860 real moves, and after K-2, per-cell (evidence × role-delta), not just on average. Extrapolations are flagged, not disguised.
  6. "What's the weakest input?" — Serving-time opponent lineups (team known, five-on-ice sampled). Standing sensitivity ablation; ask for its number when the tower trains.
  7. "Does any of this delay October?" — No. GPU is the only gating resource, and v0 is the floor either way.
