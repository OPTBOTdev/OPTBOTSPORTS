The Phases, in Plain Language — and what counts as MVP

Read this so every audit decision you make connects to WHAT we're shipping and WHEN. The product: "tell an NHL team what a player becomes on THEIR team, before he plays there — with honest error bars." Everything below serves that one sentence.

What MVP means here: enough proof to land a partnership with an NHL team or seed funding — nothing more. Concretely that proof is: (1) the projection engine — today's v0 already beats the industry by 7.6%, hopefully upgraded to v1 (trained Phase A+B) if it wins the shootout; (2) sealed predictions for every 2026-27 mover, hash-locked in September; (3) the public scoreboard + demo that let anyone watch the proof happen live. The triage rule for any feature question: does it strengthen THAT proof before October? If not, it's post-MVP no matter how cool.

PHASE A — the Environment Tower  ("describe the ice around him")

The point: a player's stats are half him, half his surroundings. Phase A is a small neural network whose ONLY job is turning "who and what surrounds a player" into one number-vector the rest of the system can use — so when we swap the surroundings (new team!), the prediction actually knows what changed. Today's v0 uses a crude average ("a typical 2nd-line environment"); Phase A replaces that with the SPECIFIC humans and situation.

Data it eats: the windows (Section 2 of the Field Guide) + the member shells + coach table + team priors. Status: coded, awaiting GPU. MVP: yes IF it beats v0 in the harness; v0 ships otherwise.

PHASE B — the Combiner  ("player + environment + coach's knobs → prediction")

The point: take three separate pipes — the environment (from A), the player's talent (from C), and the coach's deployment knobs (zone starts, matchup exposure) — and combine them into predicted outcomes. The pipes are kept physically separate inside the network so "how good he is" can never get tangled with "how he was used" — that separation IS the company's core trick.

Data it eats: A's output + C's talent numbers + the 17 lever columns + bio; trains against MoneyPuck xG targets on 6.5M windows. Status: coded, awaiting GPU. MVP: same deal as A — ships only if it beats v0.

PHASE C — the Player Side  ("who IS he, separated from where he played")

The point: measure each player's true contribution by comparing what he produced to what an average player would've produced in his exact situations — career-long, recent-games-weighted, pulled toward average when evidence is thin. This number is already computed for everyone and is the engine of the proven 7.6%. Later (v2), it grows a "style" dimension (HOW he produces: rush sniper vs net-front vs point bomber — we've pre-computed 13,708 shooting-personality cards for this) which, combined with Phase A's people-vectors, becomes the chemistry/fit engine.
